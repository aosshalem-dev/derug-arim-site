## B4. Ideology & Programs

### Core Methodology: "Status-Based Aggression" Model
The central theoretical claim: "Much of aggression among children and youth stems from a desire to improve and maintain their social status among peers." The intervention aims to reduce social incentives for popular students engaging in aggressive behavior. When groups stop rewarding harmful conduct, phenomena like rumor-spreading, social ostracism, and group divisions decrease. [Source: A4, A7]

Key academic reference: Research by American sociologist Dr. Robert Faris (UC Davis) on how popular youth use social power to marginalize peers. [Source: A5]

### Intellectual Sources
- **Gewalt Akademie Villigst (Germany):** Violence prevention academy in Westphalia where co-founder Rossler trained. [Source: A5]
- **AVP (Alternatives to Violence Project, USA):** Peace-building and conflict resolution workshops. [Source: A3, A4]
- **IIRP (International Institute for Restorative Practices, USA):** Restorative justice methodology. [Source: A3, A4]
- **Prof. Robert Faris (UC Davis):** Status-based aggression research. [Source: A3, A5]

### Program Portfolio

1. **3-Day Intensive Workshop (flagship):** Held over 3 consecutive full school days in the student's regular classroom. Uses group dynamics, games, and exercises with the classroom teacher present. Aims to expose authentic behaviors and shift group norms. Grades 4-12. [Source: A4, A11]

2. **Cyberbullying Session:** Single-day workshops on online aggression for grades 4-12. [Source: A4]

3. **Educator Training:** Annual certification courses through Pisga training centers and university partnerships. Over 10,000 educators trained. Taught at Ben-Gurion University, Kinneret College, Ornit College, Heidelberg University. [Source: A4, A5]

4. **"Matzmichim in Sports":** Adaptation for sports teams. [Source: A3 - staff listing]

5. **Military/Business/Elderly adaptations:** The organization expanded beyond schools to businesses, military units, sports teams, academic institutions, and senior housing. [Source: A3]

6. **Post-Oct 7 Trauma Support:** Pivoted to support evacuated student populations, teachers managing trauma in classrooms, and Arab-Israeli communities dealing with criminal violence. [Source: A6]
