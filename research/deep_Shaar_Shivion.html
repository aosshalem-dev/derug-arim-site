1. Organizational Structure & Legal Identity

1.1 Legal Entity

  • Name: שער שוויון (ע"ר) -- Sha'ar Shivyon Registered Association
  • Original Name: "The Israeli Sports and Education Initiative" (registered May 2012)
  • Name Change: October 2021, renamed to שער שוויון
  • Registration Number: 580558591
  • Legal Type: Amuta (registered association)
  • Address: Ben Tzvi Yitzhak 6, Hod HaSharon, 7537303
  • Field: Sports
  • Status: Active, proper management (Certificate of Proper Management 2022), Article 46 tax-exempt status (2024-2026)
  • Midot Effectiveness Badge: Valid through 03/2026 (awarded by Midot/SFI Group)

1.2 Founding Story

The organization was founded in 2009 by Liran Gerassi (born 1985) while he was a student at Hebrew University of Jerusalem. Gerassi volunteered at a community center in Jerusalem's Talpiot neighborhood and observed youth engaging in destructive behaviors during after-school hours. He gathered them to form a soccer team, secured sponsorship for equipment, and paired athletic training with academic tutoring. The model proved effective -- teachers noticed improved academic performance, leading schools to adopt the framework.

  • Started with 7 soccer teams from 7 schools in 2009
  • Registered as NGO in 2012/2013
  • Expanded to high schools in 2014
  • Ministry of Education inclusion by 2017
  • By 2020: 300 teams, ~4,500 participants
  • By 2025/2026: 410+ teams, 10,000+ participants

1.3 Relationship to JDC/Joint Israel

CRITICAL CORRECTION: The prior cluster research report stated that "שער שוויון פועלת תחת ג'וינט ישראל" (operates under Joint Israel). This claim is not supported by evidence.

Evidence against JDC subordination:

  • שער שוויון is registered as an independent association (amuta), not as a JDC program
  • The organization's website, GuideStar listing, and obudget entry make no reference to JDC
  • The founder (Liran Gerassi) is an independent social entrepreneur, not a JDC employee
  • The board of directors has no identified JDC representatives
  • Partners listed include: The Jewish Agency, Harris Philanthropies, Wingate Institute, Decathlon, Jerusalem Foundation -- but NOT JDC

What was likely confused: The program "סודקות את תקרת הזכוכית" (Cracking the Glass Ceiling) IS a JDC program. The prior research appears to have merged data from שער שוויון and סודקות into a single "feminist cluster" analysis, incorrectly attributing JDC connections to שער שוויון.

4. Programs & Curriculum Content

4.1 Core Programs

1. Sha'ar Shivyon (The Equalizer) -- Flagship program

  • 270 groups, 4,590 participants
  • Soccer + learning centers (homework help, test prep)
  • 4 sessions/week: 2 soccer practice, 2 study center
  • Monthly regional tournaments
  • Ages: 4th-10th grade
  • Mixed gender, mixed ethnicity (Jewish, Arab, Bedouin, Druze, Ethiopian, immigrant)
  • Condition: Participants must maintain good school conduct and attendance

2. Boatot (בועטות) -- Girls' soccer

  • 95 groups, 1,615 girls
  • Focus: Self-confidence, physical self-image, belonging
  • 60 tournaments annually
  • Partnership with Ministry of Culture and Sports, Football Association
  • Expanded to youth villages (residential education)

3. The 48ers -- Basketball

  • 25 groups, 500 teens
  • Created 2016 in partnership with Josh Harris (Philadelphia 76ers)
  • Basketball + STEM education + leadership
  • 32 tournaments yearly
  • Named after Israel's 1948 founding (parallel to 76ers for 1776)

4. Female Empowerment -- Girls' basketball

  • 12 teams, 200 players
  • Basketball + weekly enrichment workshops
  • Focus: Gender empowerment, self-confidence, body image, belonging
  • Israeli First Lady Michal Herzog participated in a tournament

5. Special Goals -- Special needs teens

  • 4 teams, 80 teens (northern region)
  • Soccer for integration and independence
  • 5 tournaments annually

6. Safe Swimmers

  • 8 groups, 320 children
  • Swimming lessons for periphery children
  • Combats drowning (major issue in Arab/Bedouin communities)

7. Bar/Bat Mitzvah Project -- Listed on website, details limited

4.2 Curriculum Analysis

The actual educational content centers on:

  1. Academic tutoring -- homework help, test preparation, coordinated with school staff
  2. Sportsmanship values -- "Fair Team Trophy" at tournaments, no red cards policy
  3. Coexistence -- bringing together children from different backgrounds through shared sports
  4. Violence prevention -- keeping youth occupied in positive after-school activities
  5. Anti-racism -- cross-cultural encounters through tournaments
  6. Self-confidence -- physical achievement, team belonging

What is NOT in the curriculum (based on available evidence):

  • Gender theory or gender identity education
  • Intersectional feminism
  • Critical pedagogy (Paulo Freire)
  • Political activism training
  • Anti-patriarchy messaging

The "gender empowerment" component in Boatot and Female Empowerment programs focuses on practical self-confidence (body image, physical capability, belonging) rather than ideological gender theory. This is closer to traditional girls' sports empowerment (like Title IX in the US) than to critical gender theory.

5. Ideology Assessment

5.1 Core Ideology

The organization's stated ideology is sport-based social change: using athletic activity as a common language to bridge social, ethnic, and economic divides. Key principles:

  • Sport as "the great equalizer"
  • Coexistence through shared experience (not through ideological education)
  • "The field is the bridge"
  • Equal access to development opportunities regardless of background

5.2 Is This Feminist/Gender-Agenda?

The prior research classified this as "Feminist/Gender-Agenda (risk: high)." This requires significant qualification:

Evidence FOR gender focus:

  • Boatot (girls' soccer) and Female Empowerment (girls' basketball) are gender-specific programs
  • The name includes "שוויון" (equality)
  • The website mentions "gender equality" alongside social and geographic equality
  • Gefen classification includes gender-related markers

Evidence AGAINST primary gender ideology:

  • The flagship program (Sha'ar Shivyon) is mixed-gender -- boys and girls together
  • The organization also runs programs for boys, special needs teens, and mixed groups
  • The founder's origin story is about violence prevention and coexistence, not gender
  • The main body of work (270 out of ~400 groups) is the mixed-gender flagship
  • Gender-specific programs (Boatot + Female Empowerment) represent ~27% of groups (107 out of ~400)
  • The "empowerment" described is sports-based confidence building, not ideological
  • No evidence of gender theory, intersectional feminism, or critical pedagogy in curriculum
  • The organization received a UEFA award for social change -- European football governance recognizing sports-based work, not gender activism

5.3 Political Positioning

Apolitical positioning is plausible:

  • Works with both Jewish and Arab communities
  • Operates in Bedouin, Druze, and mixed cities
  • Post-October 7 context: continued Arab-Jewish programming during war (Israel21c reported)
  • Post-May 2021: expanded to mixed cities (Lod, Haifa, Acre) after ethnic clashes
  • Recognition from UEFA, Israeli Football Association
  • Tottenham Hotspur captain Hugo Lloris: "It is clear that the charity does a fantastic job in bringing together Israeli and Palestinian children through the power of football"
  • Israeli First Lady Michal Herzog participated in a tournament

Potential concern areas:

  • The coexistence model could be viewed as normalizing/depoliticizing structural inequalities
  • The Revson Foundation grant specifically funds "Arab and Bedouin communities" -- this is sector-targeted
  • The "social change" language could evolve over time as the organization grows
  • Any organization working in Arab-Jewish relations is inherently political in the Israeli context

5.4 Assessment: Pure Merit-Based vs. Intersectional

More merit-based than intersectional. The model is:

  • Sport as equalizer (meritocratic -- performance matters on the field)
  • Academic tutoring (practical, achievement-oriented)
  • Coexistence through shared activity (contact theory, not ideology)
  • Condition-based participation (you must behave well in school to play)

This is fundamentally different from a critical-theory approach that would frame society as oppressive and seek to create "agents of change."

10. Open Questions

  1. Who exactly comprises the 3.9M NIS in foreign donations? Harris (~1M+) and Revson (~300K) account for ~1.3M+, leaving ~2.6M from unidentified foreign donors. Are these additional Jewish federations?
  1. What is in the "other" 6M NIS revenue category? Likely corporate partnerships and program fees, but not publicly itemized.
  1. What is the actual content of the "social-values" sessions? The 721,014 NIS in the Gefen mapping is for "social-values" programming. We know it includes anti-racism, coexistence, and sportsmanship -- but what specific materials are used?
  1. Has the curriculum content shifted over time? As the organization has grown from 7 teams to 410+, has there been mission drift toward more ideological content?
  1. David Bernstein (International President) -- who is he? Common name; requires LinkedIn/professional verification.
  1. Effie Rosenhaus (Honorary President) -- what is his background? May provide insight into the organization's donor network.
  1. What is the relationship between Liran Gerassi's "Global GameChangers" organization and שער שוויון?
  1. The Revson Foundation grant specifically targets Arab/Bedouin communities. Does this create a demographic focus that could shift the organization's mission over time?
