1. What Is This Organization?

Full name: האקדמיה למדעי ההפסקה (The Academy for Recess Sciences / The Academy for Break Sciences)

Also known as: האקדמיה ללימודי ההפסקה (variant name on Holam/Toval platform)

Tagline: "הדור החדש של ההפסקות" (The New Generation of Breaks)

Website: games-academia.co.il

Nature: An educational startup/initiative focused on improving school recess through structured peer-led games. Not a traditional NGO or amuta. Appears to be a sole proprietorship or small company run by a single founder.

App: "Playlist Game" — available on Google Play and iOS. Contains 1,000+ social games and 500 conversation-starter questions.

Commercial products: Card game sets (₪120 per family set, 70 game cards + 70 question cards). Bulk discounts available.

2. What Do They Actually Do During Breaks?

The core model:

  1. Selection of student coaches: Teachers identify older students — often specifically those who are socially isolated ("transparent" students) — to become "social coaches" (מאמנים חברתיים) or "break leaders" (מובילי הפסקה).
  1. Training: These students are trained to lead creative games during recess. Games are:
  • Equipment-free (no special equipment needed)
  • Non-competitive (no winners or losers)
  • Simple (3-sentence instructions + demo video)
  • Original (developed by the Academy)
  1. Implementation: Fixed activity stations during breaks, run by trained student coaches without teacher involvement. Coaches also identify isolated children and draw them in.
  1. Classroom extension: Teachers can integrate the games into classroom instruction.
  1. Festival/event format: Schools can host "game festivals" exposing all students to diverse games.

Philosophy: "We give them fishing rods, not fish" — the approach teaches students self-directed engagement rather than providing direct adult assistance. Break time becomes "life in miniature" — a practice ground for social skills.

4. Does "Leadership" (מנהיגות) Embed Specific Ideological Content?

Short answer: No. This is a false positive.

The "leadership" keyword in the context of this program refers to:

  • Peer leadership skills — older students learning to organize activities for younger ones
  • Self-confidence and initiative — listed alongside concentration, emotional intelligence, social skills
  • "כושר מנהיגות" (leadership capacity) — one of several life skills the games claim to develop

The full list of values/skills the program promotes:

  • Self-confidence (ביטחון עצמי)
  • Leadership capacity (כושר מנהיגות)
  • Willpower (כוח רצון)
  • Concentration (ריכוז)
  • Emotional intelligence (אינטליגנציה רגשית)
  • Social skills (מיומנויות חברתיות)
  • Empathy (אמפתיה)
  • Excellence (מצוינות)
  • Acceptance of diversity (קבלת השונה)

No political, religious, national, gender, or social-justice ideology was found anywhere — not on the website, not in news articles, not in the Gefen catalog description, not in school implementations. The values are generic prosocial skills. The founder's book on the Bible is non-denominational and academic in nature, endorsed by a religious-Zionist institution (Herzog College), but this is entirely separate from the school program.

The Leon Family Foundation (קרן משפחת ליאון) references the Academy's approach in its educational practices collection — specifically in a "break time mediation" (תיווך זמני הפסקה) practice guide — but only as an external video resource, not as a partner or funder.

12. Risk Summary

Revised risk rating: 2/5 (down from initial 3/5)

Risks identified:

  1. Legal structure opacity — likely a sole proprietorship without amuta transparency requirements. No GuideStar listing. Financial data not publicly available. (Low concern given the tiny budget of ₪15,900)
  2. One-person dependency — entire program relies on a single founder. No evident organizational depth or succession plan.
  3. Drushim.co.il discrepancy — listed as amuta with 11-50 employees, but no amuta registration found. This is either an error or a classification the founder chose on the platform.

Risks NOT identified:

  • No ideological embedding
  • No political agenda

  • No financial irregularities detectable at this scale
  • No complaints, criticism, or controversies found in any source
  • "Leadership" keyword is a false positive

13. Open Questions / Further Investigation

  1. Legal entity type: Is this actually registered as an amuta? Or is it a sole proprietorship / company? The drushim.co.il classification and the actual GuideStar absence need resolution. A direct check of the Companies Register (רשם החברות) or the Amutot Register (רשם העמותות) could clarify.
  2. Epoch Times connection depth: The 2016 award from Epoch Times Israel is noted. While Epoch Times Israel is not the same as the Falun Gong-affiliated international edition, the connection could be worth a surface-level check.
  3. Actual number of schools served over time: The Gefen budget shows 5 schools, but the broader footprint (mentioned cities, testimonials) suggests more schools over the years, possibly funded through direct payments or other channels.
  4. Shai Piron endorsement context: Was this given in a personal or official capacity? When exactly?
