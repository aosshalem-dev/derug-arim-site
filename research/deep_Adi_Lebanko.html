3. Program Content Analysis: "21st Century Learning Strategies"

What the Program Actually Teaches

Based on all available evidence, the program covers purely academic/study skills:

  • Memory function and mechanisms — how encoding, storage, and retrieval work
  • Information organization — methods for summarizing, note-taking, processing material
  • Learning style identification — recognizing individual learning preferences
  • Time management — scheduling, avoiding procrastination, task prioritization
  • Study techniques — preparation for exams, continuous study habits vs. cramming
  • Reading comprehension and writing skills — basic academic literacy
  • Metacognition — teaching students to think about how they think and learn
  • Self-regulated learning — taking responsibility for one's own learning process

What the Program Does NOT Contain (no evidence found)

  • No values/ideological content — zero references to democracy, equality, social justice, Jewish values, identity, or any political themes
  • No social-emotional learning (SEL) — no mention of emotional regulation, empathy, social skills, relationship building
  • No gender/sexuality content
  • No civics/citizenship content
  • No peace/coexistence content
  • No religious or anti-religious content
  • No environmental/sustainability messaging

The "21st Century" Branding — Analysis

The phrase "אסטרטגיות למידה המאה ה-21" (21st Century Learning Strategies) in the program name warrants scrutiny because "21st century skills" is a loaded term in Israeli education discourse. However:

The term as used by Levenko appears to be marketing language rather than ideological alignment. His actual content focuses on traditional study skills (memory, time management, exam prep) repackaged with contemporary framing. This is common among Gefen suppliers who need to align their program names with MoE buzzwords to attract school purchases.

The broader "21st century skills" landscape in Israel:

  • The Israeli MoE adopted 21st century skills frameworks based on P21 (Partnership for 21st Century Learning) and OECD models
  • The P21 "4Cs" framework: Critical thinking, Creativity, Collaboration, Communication
  • The MoE's "דמות הבוגר" (Graduate Profile) 2020-2030 incorporates these skills alongside values like "commitment to the values of the State of Israel as a Jewish and democratic state"
  • SEL (Social-Emotional Learning) has become increasingly integrated into MoE policy (שפ"י — SHEFI department)
  • Academic research (e.g., "Adapting the Education System to 21st Century Skills: The Case of Israel," EU-JER) notes a gap between declarative policy and actual implementation

Key distinction: The broader MoE "21st century" framework does include values and SEL components at the policy level. But Levenko's specific program focuses on the cognitive/metacognitive skills subset only — study techniques, memory strategies, time management. He does not operate in the SEL or values space.

Connection to OECD/UNESCO Frameworks

  • OECD Education 2030 / Learning Compass — Israel participates in this framework, which emphasizes competencies for students to "navigate toward well-being"
  • UNESCO Education 2030 / SDG 4 — Quality education for all
  • P21 Framework — The predominant model in Israeli MoE policy documents
  • Levenko's connection: Superficial/branding only. His program content is vanilla study-skills training that could have existed unchanged in 1995. The "21st century" label is contemporary packaging.

4. Network and Reach

How Did One Individual Build a 10-City Network?

This is actually not remarkable for a Gefen supplier. The mechanism is straightforward:

  1. Gefen catalog inclusion — Once approved as a Gefen supplier, any school in Israel can select your program using their allocated Gefen budget. Schools browse the catalog and choose.
  2. Low unit cost — At ~₪1,647 per school, this is likely a single workshop day. Schools face minimal risk in trying it.
  3. College lecturing network — Levenko lectures at Oranim, Herzog, Levinsky-Wingate, and Pisga centers throughout the country. These are exactly the venues where school principals and teachers encounter new Gefen suppliers. This creates organic word-of-mouth.
  4. Nitzan Association connection — Working with Israel's largest learning-disabilities association gives access to a nationwide network of schools serving LD students.
  5. Padlet/digital presence — Evidence of using digital tools (Padlet boards, Calameo publications) to share materials with workshop participants, creating ongoing engagement.

Assessment: 52 schools across 10 cities is modest reach for a Gefen supplier. The largest suppliers operate in hundreds of schools. This is consistent with a sole-proprietor operation.
