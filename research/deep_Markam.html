What Is "סיור פני אדם" (Human Faces Tour)?

The program is an immersive audio headphone tour inside the former "Pussycat" (הפוסיקט) strip club at the Colosseum building, Kikar Atarim (Atarim Square), Tel Aviv.

What happens during the tour:

  1. Students receive headphones and enter the three-story building (including underground levels)
  2. They listen to an audio installation created by artist Alit Kraiz (עלית קרייז), based on a concept by the company "Otzarot" (אוצרות)
  3. The audio tells the stories of women who worked at the strip club
  4. Different tours are adapted for male and female participants
  5. Students walk through the actual rooms of the former club
  6. After the audio tour, a processing workshop addresses: choice, responsibility, sexuality, boundaries, digital responsibility, healthy sexuality, gender equality
  7. The program explicitly aims at education for healthy sexuality, violence reduction, risk behavior prevention, and social responsibility

Source: https://www.2school.co.il/InterData/etsmh/ (Gefen catalog), https://mishmarhinuch.com/gefen/ (Mishmar HaChinuch database)

The Building's History

  • The Colosseum building at Kikar Atarim was a notorious strip club ("הפוסיקט") that opened in 2012
  • It was closed in 2019 after the JTLV real estate company (led by Amir Biram, Ariel Roter, and investor Shalom Fisher) purchased the property and refused to renew the lease
  • JTLV donated the building to "המרחב החברתי" for use as a social center
  • All profits from the Social Space are directed toward funding professional training courses for women survivors of prostitution, sexual violence, and economic abuse

Source: https://www.ynet.co.il/activism/article/BJk900lUE00

5. IDEOLOGY: "Mixed Communities" — What Does It Mean?

Entity A (the original מרקם)

"Mixed communities" means religious-secular Jewish coexistence — NOT Jewish-Arab. This is specifically about religious (dati) and secular (hiloni) Jews choosing to live together in the same neighborhoods, share community institutions, and create a joint Jewish lifestyle.

The movement emerged from Gesher (a religious-secular bridge organization) and Tzav Piyus (reconciliation initiative). Founded after tensions around religious-secular divides in Israeli society. Over 20 communities across Israel participate.

Connected to: Neemanei Torah VeAvodah (Religious Zionist moderate movement), MAKOM (national council of mission-driven communities), Meitarim network.

Entity B (The Social Space / Gefen program)

The ideology is gender justice, anti-trafficking, feminist activism. The program uses the former strip club as a pedagogical tool — the building itself becomes evidence of exploitation. Students experience what the space felt like and then process the moral implications.

Key ideological claims:

  • Education for "healthy sexuality" (מיניות בריאה)
  • Anti-prostitution / anti-trafficking perspective
  • Gender equality advocacy
  • Social responsibility and "involved citizenship"

Entity C (מרקם חברתי)

Social equality via legal education workshops in schools. Minimal ideological footprint.

This is a transparency gap: a 3.37M operation with 95% private funding and no public donor list.

8. CONTROVERSIES & MEDIA

No Major Controversies Found

  • The Social Space (Entity B) experienced false rumors about a plan to establish a synagogue in the former strip club — these were debunked
  • The Pussycat club's closure itself was controversial (closure of a strip club raises free-market vs. exploitation debates)
  • Mishmar HaChinuch (Guardian of State Education) — a watchdog organization founded by Givat Haviva, IDEA, and others — includes the "סיור פני אדם" program in its database of Gefen programs with ideological content, alongside programs by Im Tirtzu, Dror Israel, and others. This is a monitoring/flagging database, not necessarily a condemnation.

Potential Criticism Areas (Not Yet Documented):

  1. Name confusion: The Gefen supplier "מרקם" creates confusion with the religious-secular network. Whether intentional or accidental, schools selecting program #31655 might assume they're getting a religious-secular dialogue program and instead receive a feminist anti-trafficking tour with sexuality content.
  2. Sexuality content for minors: The program involves walking through a former strip club and discussing sexuality, boundaries, and digital responsibility with grades 9-13. Some parents or communities may object.
  3. Gender-differentiated tours: The fact that male and female participants receive different audio tours raises questions about the pedagogical approach and messaging.
  4. Financial opacity: NIS 3.37M revenue with 95% private funding and no public donor list.
