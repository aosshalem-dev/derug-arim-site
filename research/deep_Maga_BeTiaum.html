Business Model

The company operates as a B2G (business-to-government) service provider:

  1. Revenue source: Schools and municipalities pay from their Gefen/supplementary education budgets
  2. Delivery method: Workshops and lectures delivered by the company's trained facilitators
  3. Per-school cost: Ranges from ~12,000 to ~50,000 NIS per school, suggesting variable scope (number of sessions, class sizes)
  4. Average per school: ~16,800 NIS (based on total 453K / 27 schools)

The Revolving Door

This is the most significant governance finding. Shiri Basin Savion's career path represents a textbook case of "regulatory capture" via revolving door:

  1. At the Ministry: She worked in the unit that approves and oversees sexuality education programs in schools
  2. At the company: She now directs a private company that delivers those same programs
  3. The advantage: She brings insider knowledge of approval processes, personal relationships with decision-makers, and understanding of what the Ministry will accept

Critical questions that remain unanswered:

  • When exactly did Shiri Basin Savion leave the Ministry of Education?
  • Did she approve or recommend מגע בתיאום programs while still at the Ministry?
  • Is there a mandatory cooling-off period for MoE employees moving to private sector education companies?
  • Did she bring any Ministry-developed materials or methodologies to the private company?

Semantic Analysis of the Core Concept

The name "מגע בתיאום" (Touch in Coordination/Consensual Touch) is itself a conceptual framework:

"הילדים לומדים את המושג 'מגע בתיאום', וזאת על מנת להשריש תרבות לפיה כל מגע בינאישי בין ילדים ובין ילדים ומבוגרים, ייעשה בתיאום תוך בקשה או שאלה"
(Children learn the concept of 'touch in coordination,' in order to instill a culture in which all interpersonal touch between children, and between children and adults, is done in coordination through a request or question)

Concern: This applies an adult consent framework (originally derived from sexual consent discourse) to all physical interactions of young children, including normal play, comforting, and spontaneous affection. While child safety is important, the universal application of "consent culture" to kindergarteners risks:

  • Creating anxiety about normal physical interactions
  • Disrupting spontaneous childhood play
  • Pathologizing normal affection between children
  • Introducing sexuality-adjacent concepts at developmentally inappropriate ages

Delivery Method

The programs use:

  • Games, videos, puzzles, and dramatized scenarios
  • Experiential/interactive format (not lecture-based)
  • "Held and safe space" (מרחב מוחזק ובטוח) -- therapeutic terminology applied to educational settings

Ministry of Education Relationship

Approval Status

The company claims its programs are "approved by the Ministry of Education" (מאושר מטעם משרד החינוך). However, there is a contradiction:

  • The 2school.co.il Gefen catalog page explicitly states: "Not part of Ministry of Education framework" (מסגרת שיוך: לא משויך)
  • Yet the company presents itself as MoE-approved on the sei.org.il listing and elsewhere

This suggests the company may have Gefen catalog listing (which allows schools to purchase programs from approved vendors) but not formal integration into the MoE's own curriculum.

The Gefen System

The Gefen (גפ"ן -- Gefen Programs Portal) is a catalog of external programs that schools can purchase. Being listed in Gefen means:

  • The program passed some review process
  • Schools can use their supplementary education budgets to pay for it
  • It does NOT necessarily mean the Ministry endorses the specific content

Approval Process Opacity

No public information is available about:

  • The specific criteria used to approve sexuality education programs for Gefen
  • Whether competitive processes exist for this category
  • Who within the Ministry reviews and approves these programs
  • Whether the reviewers have ideological alignment with the providers
  • Whether alternative approaches (religious, conservative, purely medical) have been offered and rejected

The Revolving Door's Impact on Approval

With Shiri Basin Savion having moved from the MoE's sexuality unit to מגע בתיאום, the company has a direct pipeline to the approval system:

  • She knows what the reviewers look for
  • She may have personal relationships with current reviewers
  • She may have helped shape the criteria that her company now meets

This represents a classic pattern of regulatory capture where the regulated entity effectively controls or heavily influences the regulatory process.

Institutional Hub: האגודה לחינוך מיני

The Israeli Association for Sex Education (sei.org.il), established March 2023 (notably, just months after מגע בתיאום's incorporation in November 2022), serves as:

  1. Legitimation body -- all member organizations "adopted the association's goals, vision, and ethical code"
  2. Self-certification system -- the association trains and certifies the facilitators who then deliver programs
  3. Network coordinator -- 28 member organizations listed in the directory

Critical finding: מגע בתיאום is listed as one of 28 member organizations. Other cluster members also listed include:

  • מידע אמין על מין (Shlomit Hebron is on the association's steering committee)
  • לדעת לבחור נכון
  • סייף סקול (SafeSchool)
  • לגעת בזה, המעורר (Touch It, The Awakener)

Personnel Overlaps

Shlomit Hebron -- co-director of מידע אמין על מין -- sits on the steering committee of the Association. This means the same people who deliver programs also govern the body that certifies them.

Network Pattern

The pattern that emerges is a distributed network with shared ideology but separate legal entities:

  • Some are amutot (tax benefits, donation-friendly)
  • Some are חברות בע"מ (less transparency, profit-taking possible)
  • One is a self-certifying professional association
  • Independent facilitators operate as freelancers within the same ecosystem

This structure makes financial tracking extremely difficult and provides multiple entry points into the school system.

Parental Consent Mechanism

The Problem

No evidence of a parental opt-in mechanism has been found. The model appears to be:

  1. School/municipality decides to purchase the program
  2. The program is delivered to students
  3. Parents may be offered "lectures" (הרצאות להורים) as a separate track
  4. Parents are NOT given the actual curriculum materials in advance
  5. Parents are NOT asked for explicit consent (opt-in)

Evidence

From the existing research:

"בכל התיאורים, ההורים מוזכרים כ'קהל יעד' להרצאות, אך: אין מידע על האם ההורים רואים את התכנים המדויקים; אין דרישה להסכמה מפורשת (opt-in) של הורים; המודל הוא: המורים והמנחים החליטו, ההורים 'מוסברים'"

From the sei.org.il listing, target populations include "parents and educational teams" -- but this refers to separate lectures FOR parents, not parental consent ABOUT what children receive.

Parental Discovery

The existing research documents cases where:

"ילדיהם בגן למדו על 'מגע בתיאום' מבלי שידעו" (Their children in kindergarten learned about 'touch in coordination' without [the parents] knowing)

This is particularly concerning for:

  • Religious families whose values may conflict with the program's framework
  • Parents who have different views on age-appropriate sexuality education
  • Parents who want to be the primary source of values-based education for their children

Legal Framework

In Israel, the Education Ministry does not require explicit parental consent for individual programs purchased through the Gefen system. The assumption is that the school administration acts in loco parentis. However:

  • Sexuality education touches on deeply held religious and moral values
  • The content goes beyond factual health education into values territory
  • International best practice increasingly requires opt-in for sexuality education

Controversies

Direct Controversies for מגע בתיאום

No direct media scandals or legal actions specifically targeting מגע בתיאום have been identified. This may be because:

Related Controversy: Or Akiva Sex Education Scandal

While not directly involving מגע בתיאום, the Or Akiva incident is highly relevant as it illustrates the risks inherent in this sector:

A sex education workshop for 7th graders in Or Akiva resulted in:

  • Parents alleging the facilitator included explicit sexual vocabulary exercises, demonstrations of masturbation, and encouragement to watch pornography
  • Gender-mixed settings (unusual for such workshops)
  • Physical touching exercises between students
  • A teacher blocking a distressed student from leaving
  • The Education Minister ordered immediate cessation of all programs from that provider
  • Investigation launched at district and central Ministry levels

Relevance to מגע בתיאום: This incident demonstrates what can happen when:

  1. External facilitators deliver sexuality content without adequate oversight
  2. There is no standardized content review
  3. Parents are not informed in advance
  4. The facilitator's personal ideology overrides age-appropriate boundaries

Potential Controversy Vectors

Based on the organizational analysis, the following risks exist:

  1. Content leakage to younger ages -- the program serves kindergarten through high school; inappropriate content migration is a risk
  2. Revolving door exposure -- if Shiri Basin Savion's Ministry-to-company transition becomes public knowledge, it could trigger regulatory scrutiny
  3. For-profit model -- parents discovering that a private company profits from delivering sexuality education to their children may react negatively
  4. Religious community backlash -- the program is marketed to both religious (ממ"ד) and secular schools, but the content appears to reflect secular/progressive values
