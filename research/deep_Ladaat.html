4. Programs & Services

A. Education Center (מרכז החינוך)

Scale: 800+ workshops per year (self-reported); ~33,000 adolescents reached since 2018; approximately 30 Jerusalem schools

Target age groups: Grades 7–12 (middle school and high school); some work with younger populations in informal settings

Workshop Topics:

  • Anatomy and puberty
  • Contraception
  • Consent and mutuality
  • Healthy relationships
  • Digital safety and social media
  • Pornography literacy
  • Body image

Languages: Hebrew and Arabic

Methodology:

  • Facilitators undergo 60-hour professional training (certified by the Israeli Association for Sex Education)
  • All facilitators must have prior educational or therapeutic experience with youth and group facilitation expertise
  • Workshops are tailored by age group and school context
  • Follow-up support available through anonymous counseling center

Training for Educators: Separate workshops for school staff covering puberty, communication skills, and attitude examination

Parent Engagement: Lectures and workshops for parents (but no documented opt-in/opt-out mechanism for parental consent regarding student workshops)

B. Counseling Center (מרכז הייעוץ)

Scale: ~6,000 consultations since 2018; ~25 volunteer professionals

Services:

  • Free, anonymous counseling in Hebrew and Arabic
  • Available via WhatsApp, phone, email, Facebook, and in-person
  • Contraception selection and usage guidance
  • Unplanned pregnancy support
  • Abortion-related counseling
  • Medical services by volunteer gynecologists
  • Mobile Counseling Unit serving homeless youth and sex workers
  • "Parlour Meetings" — informal workshops in women's homes

Access: Available to all persons regardless of gender, age, marital status, place of residence, religion, or nationality. Services provided without parental consent for minors.

5. Ideology & Content Analysis

Core Ideological Framework

Ladaat explicitly states four foundational principles:

  1. Access to reliable information about sexuality and reproduction
  2. Freedom of self-expression regarding sexuality and relationships without fear
  3. Autonomy in choices regarding relationships, sexuality, and reproduction
  4. Consent as the basis for all sexual behavior — encompassing desire, mutual respect, and knowledge

The organization promotes "education for mutuality, equality, body literacy, and informed decision-making" (from SEI listing).

Progressive Positioning

Ladaat's framework is standard progressive/comprehensive sex education aligned with WHO and UNESCO guidelines. Key markers:

  • Consent-centered approach
  • Body autonomy and bodily literacy
  • Critical thinking about gender norms
  • Pornography literacy (not abstinence from pornography but critical engagement)
  • Reproductive rights advocacy (pro-choice stance clearly articulated through Forum 21)

LGBTQ+ Content

Not explicitly detailed on the organization's public-facing materials. The SEI association page lists goals including "creating social discourse and promoting political thinking on issues of sexual health and the factors that shape perceptions about sexuality, masculinity, femininity, and gender roles." The Instagram account (@ladaat.choose.well) likely includes LGBTQ+-affirming content based on the organization's progressive positioning and SEI membership, but specific curricula or workshop outlines addressing sexual orientation or gender identity were not found in public sources.

Comparison: Meyda Amin explicitly includes LGBTQ+ affirmation and gender identity exploration in its curriculum. Ladaat is more cautious in its public marketing, possibly because of its Jerusalem location and diverse constituency including Arab communities.

Formal Connections to Meyda Amin and Maga BeTiaum

  • All three are SEI members sharing its "vision, goals, and code of ethics"
  • The Maga BeTiaum research report (batch 2) explicitly identified Ladaat as part of "the cluster" alongside Meyda Amin
  • SEI serves as a self-certifying professional body — Ladaat's facilitators are trained to SEI standards, and SEI membership is held by all three Gefen sex-ed providers
  • Shiri Basin Savion (Maga BeTiaum's professional director) moved from the Ministry of Education's sexuality unit — this revolving door touches the same ecosystem that certifies Ladaat's facilitators

Key Distinction

Unlike Meyda Amin (sole proprietorship) and Maga BeTiaum (private company), Ladaat is the only registered amuta in this cluster with:

  • Public GuideStar filings
  • A named board of directors
  • Transparent financial reporting requirements
  • Nearly 50 years of institutional history

7. Controversies & Criticisms

Direct Controversies

No major public controversies found targeting Ladaat specifically. Unlike Meyda Amin (whose founder's "Achat Mitoch Achat" database of 1,800 alleged perpetrators drew attention), Ladaat has maintained a relatively low-controversy profile over its 48-year history.

Indirect/Contextual Controversies

  1. Conservative pushback on sex education generally: The November 2025 "Until Marriage" (עד החתונה) Knesset conference — organized by MK Limor Son Har Melekh's lobby — represents the conservative counter-movement. Dina Shalev's public Ynet rebuttal placed Ladaat squarely in the political crosshairs of this debate.
  1. Gender Critical Israel (ג'נדר קריטיקל ישראל): This website publishes critical content about gender-ideology education in schools. While Ladaat is not specifically named in their critiques (as far as discoverable), the broader sex-ed ecosystem it belongs to is under scrutiny from this perspective.
  1. Haredi community exclusion: The Shomrim investigative report documents that ~400,000 Haredi children receive zero sex education. Ladaat's Jerusalem location places it at the geographic center of this tension, but it does not appear to operate in Haredi schools.
  1. Reproductive rights advocacy as political activism: Ladaat's leadership of Forum 21 (the coalition to abolish pregnancy termination committees) and its explicit pro-choice advocacy through Knesset engagement positions it as a political actor, not merely an educational provider. This dual role is relevant for parents evaluating whether the organization delivers neutral education or ideologically driven content.
  1. Services to minors without parental consent: The counseling center explicitly provides services "without parental consent for minors." While this is standard practice for sexual health counseling in many Western countries, it may concern parents who expect to be informed of their children's engagement with sexuality-related services.

No documented:

  • Lawsuits
  • State Comptroller findings
  • Parent complaint campaigns
  • Media scandals
  • Financial irregularities

8. Scope & Geographic Reach

Jerusalem Core

  • ~30 schools in Jerusalem (per English website)
  • 800+ workshops per year across these schools and other frameworks
  • 33,000 adolescents reached since 2018 (cumulative)
  • 6,000 consultations provided since 2018

Beyond Schools

The organization operates in:

  • Secondary schools (7th–12th grade)
  • Hostels (מעונות)
  • NGO frameworks
  • At-risk youth programs (מסגרות נוער בסיכון)
  • Boarding schools (פנימיות)
  • Municipal services
  • IDF courses
  • Community centers
  • Informal education settings
  • Women's homes ("Parlour Meetings")
  • Street outreach (Mobile Counseling Unit for homeless youth and sex workers)

Arab Community Reach

Ladaat explicitly serves Arabic-speaking populations:

  • Arabic Instagram account
  • Arabic counseling services (led by Bian Ragabi and Fatina Husseini)
  • Arabic workshops in formal and informal settings

National Reach via Digital

The organization's social media presence (Instagram, TikTok) extends its reach nationally beyond its Jerusalem physical footprint.

Gefen Footprint

Gefen program #992 is listed in the Ministry of Education's catalog, allowing any school in Israel to select it. However, the actual deployment appears concentrated in Jerusalem and surrounding areas. The exact number of cities and schools using program #992 through Gefen was not found in public mapping data.

12. Open Questions for Further Investigation

  1. Specific curricula: What exactly is taught in each workshop? Lesson plans are not publicly available. A parent seeking to review content before their child participates would have difficulty doing so.
  1. LGBTQ+ content specifics: The organization's position on gender identity education is not explicitly stated in public materials. Does program #992 include gender identity content? Sexual orientation content? At what ages?
  1. Gefen deployment data: How many schools outside Jerusalem use program #992? The Gefen mapping data would show this.
  1. NIF/Shatil connection: No evidence of NIF funding was found, but the overlap in advocacy goals (reproductive rights, progressive education) and the use of PEF as fiscal sponsor (a common NIF-adjacent funding channel) warrants investigation.
  1. Forum 21 partners: Which other organizations are in the Forum 21 coalition? This would map the full advocacy network.
  1. Board member backgrounds: Dr. Shulamit Shulov-Barkan (chair) and Gusti Yehoshua Braverman appear to be notable figures. Their full backgrounds would illuminate the organization's governance orientation.
  1. Mobile Counseling Unit: What content is delivered to homeless youth and sex workers? Who funds this operation?
  1. Arabic programming: What specific content is delivered to Arab communities in East Jerusalem? Is it identical to the Hebrew curriculum or adapted?
