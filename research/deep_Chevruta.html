Rabbi Ariel Dorfman - Founder & CEO

  • Role: Founder and Director of Chevruta / HaMakom
  • Background: Gush Katif evacuee (2005 disengagement); relocated to Tel Aviv as part of the Religious-Zionist settlement kernel movement
  • Residence: Reported in Pisgat Ze'ev (East Jerusalem settlement neighborhood)
  • Education: Studied at multiple yeshivot including Noam, Netiv Meir, Yeshivat HaKotel, Merkaz HaRav, Kfar Chassidim
  • Additional career: Cantorial performer; has released albums of liturgical music including settings of Rabbi Kook's poetry and Zelda's poetry
  • Teaching: Teaches courses on Rabbi Abraham Isaac Kook's writings at yeshiva.org.il
  • Ideology: Religious-Zionist; follower of Rabbi Kook's philosophy of Jewish national redemption; affiliated with Tekuma party (part of Religious Zionist party)
  • Family: Married, father of five; his brother Meir Dorfman also teaches at HaMakom
  • Views on LGBTQ: According to kamoha.org.il, stated his approach to homosexuality is "the mainstream of Judaism" (implying traditional Orthodox opposition)

Key quote from Chotam profile: Dorfman's distinguishing characteristic is his conviction that Jewish engagement requires persistent effort -- he insists on hosting programming even when surveys show limited awareness among target audiences.

4. IDEOLOGY: "JEWISH AWARENESS" IN PRACTICE

What "Jewish Awareness" Actually Means

Despite the neutral-sounding name, Chevruta's ideology is firmly Religious-Zionist, specifically within the school of Rabbi Abraham Isaac Kook (Rav Kook). Key indicators:

  1. Torah kernel model (garin torani): The organization is structured as a mission-driven community of Religious-Zionist families who settle in predominantly secular neighborhoods to spread Jewish observance and strengthen Jewish identity.
  1. Rav Kook's philosophy: Dorfman explicitly teaches Kook's writings. In Kook's framework, secular Jews are unwitting carriers of holiness who need to be reconnected to their spiritual roots -- the secular-religious encounter is itself part of the messianic process of national redemption.
  1. "Return to Judaism" (hazara b'tshuva) framing: The Rosh Yehudi connection is explicit -- Rosh Yehudi's founder Israel Zeira stated they want to be "Zionist Chabadniks" and are "proud of our religiousization" (hadatah). Dorfman is described as coming to Tel Aviv "to spread the Torah of Rav Kook."
  1. Settlement ideology: Funding from the Settlement Division, board member from the Smotrich family, founder from Beit El, connection to Komemiyut movement -- all point to a Religious-Zionist settlement ideology that views settling in Tel Aviv as a form of "internal settlement."
  1. Post-October 7 shift: Since the war, HaMakom explicitly pivoted from personal empowerment to national identity questions -- "what it means to be Jewish," "why we're here in Israel," "why Jews are persecuted." This aligns with Religious-Zionist framing of the conflict as existential-spiritual.

NOT Progressive / NOT Pluralist / NOT Post-Zionist

The organization is the opposite of progressive Judaism, pluralism, or post-Zionism:

  • It is Orthodox in religious orientation
  • Its leaders are settlers or settlement-affiliated
  • Its board includes a member of the Smotrich family
  • It is connected to the far-right Komemiyut movement
  • It teaches Rav Kook's religious-national philosophy
  • Its funding comes heavily from settlement-oriented government ministries

The "awareness" in "Jewish Awareness" is closer to kiruv (religious outreach) within a Religious-Zionist frame than to liberal/pluralist Jewish identity exploration.

6. NETWORK CONNECTIONS

Rosh Yehudi (ראש יהודי)

  • Founder: Israel Zeira (ישראל זעירא)
  • Connection: Dorfman is described as having come to Tel Aviv within the "Rosh Yehudi" kernel framework
  • Ideology: Explicitly Religious-Zionist kiruv; Zeira stated "we want to be Zionist Chabadniks" and is "proud of our religiousization"
  • Controversies: Zeira was found to be living illegally in a synagogue basement he converted to a residence; Tel Aviv municipality initiated legal proceedings in 2024
  • Note: Rosh Yehudi is a separate organization but shares the same physical and ideological ecosystem

MAKOM Council of Mission-Driven Communities (חיבור חדש)

  • The Chevruta-HaMakom listing appears on the "Chibur Hadash" (New Connection) website, which is the council/umbrella organization for mission-driven communities (garinim) in Israel
  • This confirms Chevruta's self-identification as a mission-driven settlement kernel

Komemiyut (קוממיות)

  • Rabbi Yair Kartman (co-founder) is a member of the management of "Komemiyut - Spirit and Strength for a Jewish Israel"
  • The Yedioth investigation alleged Chevruta served as an operational arm for the Komemiyut caucus, facilitating legislative initiatives while obscuring ideological motivations
  • Komemiyut is a far-right Religious-Zionist movement

Smotrich Family Connection

  • Board member Akiva Smotrich is the brother of Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich
  • This creates a direct connection to the most senior Religious-Zionist politician in Israel's government

7. GOVERNMENT TIES AND FUNDING MECHANISMS

Ministry of Education (Largest Funder)

  • 6.3M+ NIS cumulative for "Jewish Culture" and "National Educational Nucleus" budget lines
  • The Torah Institutions Department within MoE provides an additional 1.7M+ NIS
  • Gefen system school programs are a small but visible part of MoE relationship

Settlement Division / Ministry of Settlement

  • 1.26M+ NIS cumulative from settlement-related budget lines
  • The Neemanei Torah V'Avodah investigation revealed that settlement funds designated for peripheral areas were directed to central Tel Aviv operations
  • Housing Ministry head of Neighborhood Rehabilitation testified that "Rabbi Dorfman circulated through the Housing Ministry calling himself head of the Torah Core Groups administration"

Budget Flow Pattern

The funding pattern shows a classic Religious-Zionist kernel financing structure:

  1. MoE funds for "Jewish culture" and "educational nucleus" -- legitimate-sounding categories
  2. Settlement Division funds for "settlement management" -- redirected to urban Torah kernel operations
  3. Housing Ministry funds for "neighborhood rehabilitation" -- used for cultural center in affluent Tel Aviv
  4. Agriculture Ministry funds for "urban settlement management" -- a creative budget line

8. FOREIGN FUNDING

No direct evidence found of funding from NIF, Abraham Fund, US Jewish federations, or other progressive/liberal funders. This is consistent with the organization's Religious-Zionist orientation -- these funders typically support pluralist or progressive organizations, not Orthodox kiruv operations.

The Charidy fundraising platform has a campaign page for HaMakom/Rabbi Dorfman (https://www.charidy.com/hamakom-6/1), suggesting private philanthropic fundraising, likely from Religious-Zionist donors in Israel and abroad.

Given the 9M+ annual revenue and ~5M government funding over 3 years, there is a significant gap that must be filled by donations. The likely sources are:

  • Religious-Zionist philanthropists in Israel
  • American Orthodox/Religious-Zionist donors
  • Charidy campaigns
  • Program fees and event revenue

9. CONTROVERSIES AND MEDIA COVERAGE

2018 Yedioth Ahronoth Investigation ("Malkahaim Movement")

The most significant investigative piece, published in Yedioth Ahronoth (Israel's largest newspaper), alleged:

  1. Fund misallocation: Settlement Division funds meant for peripheral, disadvantaged communities were channeled to Chevruta in affluent central Tel Aviv
  2. Political connections: The organization served as an operational arm for the Komemiyut political caucus
  3. Personal enrichment: Rabbi Dorfman and his wife received "hundreds of thousands of shekels" in salaries; Rabbi Kartman personally loaned the org money
  4. Financial distress: Despite government funding, the org reported severe deficits and took extensive loans in 2015

Neemanei Torah V'Avodah Investigation

This Religious-Zionist watchdog organization published findings about the Housing Ministry's 110M NIS annual allocation to settlement kernels, specifically noting that "the Housing Ministry admits they have no idea how 100 million shekels transferred to the Settlement Division were distributed." Chevruta/Dorfman were specifically named as recipients of 700K NIS/year from this opaque process.

"Religiousization" (Hadatah) Debate

The broader ecosystem of Torah kernels in secular cities -- including Chevruta's operations -- is a central topic in Israel's "religiousization" debate. Critics argue these organizations receive government funds to promote Orthodox religious observance in secular communities under the guise of "Jewish culture." Defenders argue they fill a genuine demand for Jewish identity content.
