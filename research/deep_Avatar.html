2. THE PROGRAM: "AVATAR — BETWEEN IMAGINATION AND REALITY"

What the Company Says:

The Avatar program is described as Wonderkids' flagship offering — an "interactive educational workshop" where:

  • Children "enter an imaginary adventure" and are "tasked with saving the world"
  • They encounter "wonderful/magical creatures"
  • They solve challenges, conduct experiments, "learn magic"
  • They are exposed to and work through personal and social content including: self-acceptance, self-confidence, social relationships, teamwork, mutual respect, responsibility-taking, and decision-making

Target Age:

  • Grades 1-2 (ages 6-8) primarily
  • Delivered in groups of approximately 15-20 children
  • Year-long curriculum with weekly sessions

Methodology:

According to TheMarker (Jan 2024) profile of founder Tal Nadler:

  • The method "leverages children's imagination as the primary resource"
  • Instructors narrate scenarios that incorporate activities, games, and various experiential elements
  • Example: Students on an "imaginary frozen planet" learned that making mistakes isn't catastrophic — they participated in "intentionally bad singing competitions" to overcome stage fright
  • The approach combines "gameplay with personal/social development themes"

What Children Actually Experience:

Based on available descriptions, the Avatar sessions involve:

  1. A narrative frame (imaginary world, quest, mission)
  2. Physical activities and games tied to the narrative
  3. "Experiments" (nature unclear — could be science demos or psychological exercises)
  4. "Magic" learning (likely stage magic/illusions)
  5. Guided discussions about feelings, self-perception, and social dynamics
  6. Group challenges requiring cooperation

The Name "Between Imagination and Reality":

This subtitle is the most concerning element. In the context of Harry Palmer's Avatar Course (see Section 7), "the space between imagination and reality" is a core philosophical concept — the idea that beliefs shape reality and that by managing one's beliefs/imagination, one can alter one's experienced reality. While the children's program may use this phrase innocuously (children exploring the boundary between fantasy play and real life), the terminological overlap is striking.

  • Per-child estimate (assuming 20 children/group, 1 group/school): ~NIS 1,570/child/year

Assessment: NIS 31,405 per school is moderate-to-high for an enrichment program. Standard Gefen-funded programs run NIS 15,000-25,000 per school. However, if the program involves multiple groups per school or specialized materials/equipment, the cost could be justified. The company's documentation of only NIS 43,000 in government transfers (and that for "circus" — not "Avatar") means the NIS 188,430 likely comes from:

  • Municipal Gefen budgets (school-level purchasing)
  • Direct school budgets
  • Parent contributions (unlikely for in-school programs)

Financial Opacity:

As a private company (בע"מ), Wonderkids Avatar Ltd. publishes zero financial information publicly. There are:

  • No annual reports
  • No donor disclosures
  • No board composition data
  • No salary information
  • No information on whether Tal Nadler or other shareholders extract profits

This is the single biggest red flag from a governance perspective. An enrichment program operating in public schools using public funds should arguably operate as an amuta with full transparency, not as a private company.

6. GOVERNMENT APPROVAL — HOW DID IT ENTER THE GEFEN CATALOG?

Gefen System Overview:

The Gefen system (גמישות פדגוגית ניהולית) allows schools to select enrichment programs from a catalog of approved providers. Approval requires:

  1. Registration as an approved supplier
  2. Program description submission
  3. Meeting basic safety and insurance requirements
  4. No rigorous content review — this is a known weakness of the system

Wonderkids in Gefen:

  • The program was not found in a direct search of the mygefen.co.il catalog (as of this research date)
  • This could mean: (a) the program uses a different catalog name, (b) it is purchased directly by schools outside Gefen, (c) it is bundled under a general "enrichment" category, or (d) it operates through municipal contracts rather than the national Gefen catalog
  • The company's obudget record shows only a "circus arts" contract — suggesting the company may list different programs under different categories

MoE Approval Status:

  • No evidence found of specific Ministry of Education approval for the "Avatar" program content
  • No evidence found of the program appearing in the MoE's official educational programs portal (apps.education.gov.il/tyhnet)
  • The company may operate entirely through local municipal purchasing, bypassing national-level content review

The Dutch Schools Scandal (2018):

In March 2018, Dutch newspaper NRC and current affairs show De Monitor revealed:

  • Avatar had infiltrated up to 6 private schools in the Netherlands
  • Schools included the Guus Kieft School (Amstelveen) and De Ruimte (Soest)
  • Schools were "democratic schools" taking in children who failed in the regular system
  • Management boards were staffed with Avatar members (six at De Ruimte)
  • Parents were encouraged to take Avatar training courses
  • Local councillors with "Wizard" status promoted Avatar by sending civil servants on courses
  • Avatar members advocated exorcism to cure cancer and ADHD in children
  • One mother withdrew her children, stating: "Our experience is that Avatar brainwashing is very evident there"
  • ~1,600 Avatar trainers operated in the Netherlands
  • The Dutch justice ministry's Sektesignaal monitoring organization asked the education minister to investigate

Ex-Member Reports:

The avataruncovered.is website and ICSA articles document:

  • Financial ruin
  • Psychological abuse
  • Hospitalizations
  • Family breakdown
  • Suicidal ideation
  • "Mind control, hypnotic exercises, confessional elements" in training
  • The Wizards Course incorporates Scientology's "Wall of Fire" narrative

The Connection Question: Is Wonderkids Avatar connected to Stars Edge?

Evidence FOR a connection:

  1. The name "Avatar" — choosing this specific name for a children's imagination/belief program is remarkable given the globally known Avatar Course
  2. "Between imagination and reality" — a core Avatar Course concept is that imagination/beliefs shape experienced reality
  3. Focus on "self-confidence through imagination" — mirrors Avatar Course's "belief management"
  4. Pardes Hanna-Karkur location — a town known for its alternative/New Age community concentration in Israel
  5. The company was incorporated in 2018 — the same year as the Dutch schools scandal. Could this represent a deliberate rebranding/distancing strategy?
  6. avatar9.co.il exists — Harry Palmer's Avatar Course is actively marketed in Israel in Hebrew
  7. No transparency on founder's training/background — "psychology and neuroscience" claims are unverifiable

Evidence AGAINST a connection:

  1. The program content as described appears to be standard SEL (social-emotional learning) delivered through imaginative play — not consciousness training
  2. "Avatar" is a common word in Hebrew pop culture (the animated series, the James Cameron films)
  3. The program also includes "magic tricks" and "experiments" — suggesting a children's entertainment approach rather than psychological techniques
  4. No direct mention of Harry Palmer, Stars Edge, "belief management," or consciousness exploration on the Wonderkids website
  5. The company offers 50+ programs — Avatar is one of many, not an exclusive focus
  6. Tal Nadler is positioned as an educator/entrepreneur, not as a spiritual teacher or Avatar Master

Assessment:

The connection cannot be confirmed, but the overlap is concerning enough to warrant investigation. At minimum, the choice of the name "Avatar" for a children's program dealing with imagination, beliefs, and self-perception — in a country where the Avatar Course is actively marketed — demonstrates either:

  • (a) Ignorance of the association (negligent)
  • (b) Deliberate choice knowing the association (alarming)
  • (c) An actual operational connection (most alarming)

The Dutch precedent demonstrates that Avatar Course adherents have historically infiltrated school systems through indirect means — not by advertising the Avatar Course directly, but by establishing educational programs that incorporate Avatar principles under different names.

11. RISK SUMMARY

Risk Factors:

  • Private company operating in public schools with zero financial transparency
  • Name identical to a globally documented consciousness-manipulation cult's core program
  • Methodology language ("between imagination and reality," belief-based self-confidence) overlaps with Avatar Course terminology
  • No published curriculum, no independent evaluation, no verifiable academic grounding
  • Founder's credentials ("psychology, neuroscience") unverifiable
  • Historical precedent: Avatar Course adherents infiltrated 6 schools in the Netherlands (2018)
  • Active Avatar Course presence in Israel (avatar9.co.il) creates possibility of cross-pollination
  • High per-school cost relative to standard enrichment programs
  • Government contract documented as "circus" — not "Avatar" — suggesting possible category mismatch

Mitigating Factors:

  • Program content as publicly described appears to be standard SEL through imaginative play
  • Company has operated for 15 years with no documented complaints
  • 100+ schools served with positive testimonials
  • 50+ programs offered — Avatar is one component, not the sole focus
  • "Avatar" is a widely used word in Israeli pop culture (animated series, films)
  • No smoking gun connecting Wonderkids to Stars Edge International

Bottom Line:

The program deserves investigation, not condemnation, based on current evidence. The most important next step is obtaining the Companies Registrar extract to identify shareholders/directors, and requesting the actual curriculum documents from the company. Parents of children in the program should ask specific questions about:

  • What exercises their children are doing
  • What "experiments" involve
  • Whether any visualization/guided imagination exercises are used
  • What training the instructors have received and from whom

If the program is genuinely just creative play with SEL themes, the name is an unfortunate coincidence. If investigation reveals connections to the Avatar Course network, this would be a serious child safety concern with national implications.
