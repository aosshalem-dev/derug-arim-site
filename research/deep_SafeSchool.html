Pattern: Regulatory Capture

The revolving door pattern here is textbook:

  1. Regulator defines the need (MoE/ShPY says schools need SEL programs)
  2. Regulator creates the procurement channel (GEFEN system)
  3. Former regulators join the company (Edri, Shadmi, Dotan, Stanescu)
  4. Former regulators bring credibility and connections (Edri as advisory board chair; Shadmi leading teacher training recognized for MoE professional development credits)
  5. Company achieves explosive growth (0 to 1,200+ schools in 4 years)

5. Product Analysis: What SafeSchool Actually Does

Three Product Layers

Layer 1: Content Streaming Platform ("The Netflix for Teachers")

  • 600+ video content units (masterclasses) with accompanying lesson plans
  • Content created by "former news and media experts and education professionals (teachers, social workers, school counselors, educational psychologists)"
  • Topics organized by age group (grades 1-14)
  • Teachers select content and design lesson plans for their classes

Layer 2: School Climate Survey System (Analytics)

  • Anonymous questionnaires administered to students
  • Measures the school's "social-emotional climate"
  • Results provided at institutional level (not individual student level)
  • Schools use data to identify areas needing intervention
  • This is the "Analytics" in the company name -- the data-driven component

Layer 3: Professional Development & Lectures

  • Physical lectures by a roster of speakers
  • Institutional teacher training courses led by Hana Shadmi (recognized for MoE professional development credits)
  • Remote broadcast system with over 1 million student participants reported
  • Content providers include former IDF Chief of Staff Aviv Kohavi, former Mossad Chief Yossi Cohen, former Justice Ministry DG Amy Palmor

Content Areas (from GEFEN catalog, program #5974)

The official GEFEN listing covers:

  • Cyberbullying and online shaming
  • Online safety hazards
  • Problematic apps
  • Substance abuse (drugs/alcohol)
  • Reckless driving and road dangers
  • Digital identity and privacy
  • Healthy sexuality
  • Financial literacy for youth
  • Positive psychology
  • Women's empowerment

Post-October 7 Pivot

After October 7, 2024, SafeSchool rapidly produced hundreds of updated lesson plans on current events and emotional resilience. The company framed this as "mass psychological treatment at scale" -- a striking term for a for-profit EdTech company. Content contributors in this period included former military/intelligence chiefs (Kohavi, Yossi Cohen).

Critical Questions

  1. Who owns the data? When SafeSchool collects climate survey data from students in 1,200+ schools, does the data belong to the school, the municipality, or the for-profit company? For an amuta there would be public accountability; for a בע"מ, there is none.
  1. Can the data be deanonymized? Even if surveys are "anonymous," if they collect grade, age, gender, and school, small class sizes could enable reidentification. No information was found about differential privacy or k-anonymity protections.
  1. What happens if the company is acquired or goes bankrupt? As a for-profit בע"מ with VC investors, the company could be acquired by any entity. What happens to the behavioral data on hundreds of thousands of Israeli children?
  1. Parental awareness: Parents can now see which GEFEN programs operate in their schools (per a Calcalist report on transparency), but the GEFEN catalog description does not make clear the scope of data collection or the sexual education content.
  1. Israeli privacy law: Israel's Privacy Protection Law (Amendment 13) requires organizations collecting personal data to appoint a Data Protection Officer and register their databases. No evidence was found that SafeSchool has publicly disclosed such compliance.

8. Government Ties and Growth Mechanism

How SafeSchool Got Into 1,200+ Schools

Step 1: Hackaveret Incubator

SafeSchool went through Hackaveret (הכוורת) -- Israel's Center for Social Innovation, co-founded by JDC Israel and the National Insurance Institute. Hackaveret provides initial funding, operating model design, and -- critically -- connections to government sectors. This is how SafeSchool gained its initial government-sector introductions.

Step 2: GEFEN System Approval

SafeSchool was approved as program #5974 in the Ministry of Education's GEFEN catalog. GEFEN (גמישות בפדגוגיה ובניהול) is the system through which schools purchase external educational programs using a NIS 2.5 billion annual allocation. Once in the catalog, SafeSchool can be purchased by any school in Israel.

Step 3: Revolving Door Personnel

With Amit Edri (former MoE DG) as advisory board chairman, Hana Shadmi (former ShPY director) leading training, and multiple former MoE officials on staff, SafeSchool has unmatched institutional credibility and connections within the education system.

Step 4: Celebrity Marketing

Gal Gadot's investment (May 2022) generated international media coverage. Rotem Sela's investment (2024) generated Israeli media coverage. These create a halo effect for school principals making purchasing decisions.

Step 5: Post-October 7 Demand Spike

The October 7 attacks and their aftermath created massive demand for emotional support content in schools. SafeSchool rapidly pivoted to produce trauma-related content, including contributions from former military/intelligence chiefs. This positioned the company as an essential service during a national crisis.

11. Risk Summary and Open Questions

High-Priority Open Questions

  1. What is the full list of shareholders and directors? Registry data requires a premium report (NIS 139-499) from webinfo.co.il or direct access to the Companies Registrar.
  1. What is the company's actual annual revenue? With 1,200+ schools paying subscription fees through GEFEN, the revenue could be substantial (estimate: if each school pays NIS 5,000-20,000/year, total revenue = NIS 6M-24M/year). But no public disclosure exists.
  1. Has there been a cooling-off period violation? Edri joined SafeSchool approximately 1 year after leaving as MoE DG. Israeli law requires a cooling-off period for senior government officials before they can work in related private sector roles. Was this complied with?
  1. Who approved SafeSchool for the GEFEN catalog, and when? The approval timeline relative to Edri's tenure as DG is critical.
  1. What specific data points are collected in the student surveys? The company describes them as "anonymous" but the actual questionnaire content and data schema are not public.
  1. Does the company have a registered database under Israel's Privacy Protection Law? Required for any entity collecting personal information on Israeli citizens.
  1. Has the Privacy Authority reviewed SafeSchool's data practices? Given the company collects behavioral/emotional data on minors at scale, this should be a regulatory priority.
  1. What parental notification and consent process exists? When a school subscribes to SafeSchool and students take climate surveys, are parents individually notified and given opt-out rights?
