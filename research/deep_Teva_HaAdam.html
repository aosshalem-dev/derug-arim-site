2. BUSINESS STRUCTURE

Registration Type: Likely Sole Proprietor (עוסק מורשה)

The Gefen catalog lists the operator as "טבע האדם / כספי אלדד" — the format "Business Name / Personal Name" is the standard listing format for a sole proprietorship (עוסק מורשה) in the Gefen system, as opposed to an amuta (association) or company (חברה בע"מ) which would be listed by their registered legal name.

No GuideStar Israel entry found — confirms this is NOT a registered amuta or public benefit company. Searching GuideStar for "טבע האדם" or "כספי" returns no matching entities.

No Companies Registrar entry found — searching for "טבע האדם" as a company name returns no results, further confirming sole proprietor status.

What This Means

  • Zero transparency obligations: Unlike amutot, sole proprietors are not required to file public financial reports, publish board member lists, or undergo audit
  • All revenue flows to a personal bank account: The 256K NIS from the Gefen program goes directly to Kaspi as personal income (minus taxes and expenses)
  • No oversight board: No independent directors, no public governance
  • This is legal and common in the Gefen system — many small educational vendors are sole proprietors — but it means there is no external accountability beyond the Ministry's own program evaluation

Similar Pattern

This matches findings from Batch 2 research (Mind Lab, Maga BeTiaum, Meyda Amin) where sole proprietorships use legal structures that avoid amuta transparency while operating programs indistinguishable from organizational activities.

Source: Gefen catalog data, GuideStar Israel search (no results), mygefen.co.il catalog structure

3. PROGRAM CONTENT

Gefen Listing

  • Program #: 1302
  • Title: תכנית להגשמה אישית וחברתית (Program for Personal and Social Fulfillment)
  • Rating: 3/5 in Gefen catalog
  • Themes tagged: Anti-racism, activism
  • Delivery: In-school workshops

What the Website Claims

The tevahadam.co.il website describes the program as dealing with "the relationship between the subconscious and conscious mind, for personal and social fulfillment." The methodology emphasizes:

  • "Freedom of choice" as the highest value
  • Individual ability to influence their environment
  • Consciousness-raising leading to tangible achievements
  • Integration of diverse knowledge domains (philosophy, social sciences, physics)
  • The individual as the primary agent of change (rather than environment determining behavior)

Methodology Assessment

The program appears to use a pop-psychology / consciousness-coaching approach wrapped in scientific-sounding language. Key methodological claims:

  1. "Where the conscious and unconscious meet" — this language borrows from psychodynamic theory but is applied in a coaching (not therapeutic) context
  2. "Scientific perspective on human thought patterns" — the "Blind Sight" workshop claims to use science to explain prejudice, but no specific methodology (CBT, contact theory, social identity theory) is cited
  3. Animal behavior theory — used to explain human psychological unrest (the "Unconscious Heaviness" workshop), which is an unusual approach
  4. "Pioneering thought for creating results and success" — motivational/coaching language

The Anti-Racism Component

The "Blind Sight" (ראיה עיוורת) workshop is the core anti-racism/anti-prejudice offering:

  • Subtitle: "Accepting the Other and Self-Acceptance"
  • Claims a "logical-emotional explanation of how people perceive themselves and others fairly and equally"
  • Claims to include a "scientific perspective on human thought patterns"
  • Participants "often reassess their worldview during the session"

Assessment: This appears to be a soft anti-prejudice workshop focused on perception and empathy, NOT a structural/political anti-racism program. It does not appear to name specific groups, address institutional racism, or engage with Israeli social-political dynamics (mizrachi/ashkenazi, Jewish/Arab, etc.). The language suggests an individualistic "we all have biases" approach rather than a systemic analysis.

The "activism" tag in the Gefen catalog likely refers to the personal empowerment / leadership development components rather than political activism per se.

Source: tevahadam.co.il/הרצאות-וסדנאות

Why This Is Notable

256K NIS is extremely high for a sole proprietor with only 10 schools. For comparison:

  • This was flagged as the second-highest budget among R3+ non-researched programs in the analysis
  • Large organizations like Matzmichim operate in 16+ cities with hundreds of schools
  • The per-school spending of ~25.6K NIS suggests intensive, multi-session programming at each school, NOT a one-time workshop

Possible Explanations

  1. Premium pricing model: If Kaspi delivers multi-session programs (the "Environment and Humanity" series is described as 4 lectures), each school engagement could reasonably cost 25K+ NIS for a full-year program
  2. Bundled services: The program may include teacher training + student workshops + individual coaching, bundling multiple Gefen line items
  3. Repeat bookings: Schools that have used the program for years may continue ordering at established price points
  4. Efficient self-marketing: As a sole proprietor with no organizational overhead (no rent for offices, no staff salaries, no board governance costs), the entire budget goes to either direct delivery or personal income

What Cannot Be Determined

  • Kaspi's actual take-home from the 256K (after materials, travel, subcontractors)
  • Whether he uses subcontractors/co-facilitators
  • How many contact hours each school receives
  • Whether the 3/5 rating reflects program quality issues

10. RISK SUMMARY & OPEN QUESTIONS

Risks

  1. Credential inflation: The website presents Kaspi as a senior expert, but his degrees are in marine biology and medical sciences (research), not education or psychology. His facilitation credentials are continuing education certificates, not clinical or academic qualifications. This does not mean the program is bad — but the presentation is misleading.
  1. Unverifiable claims: "Tens of thousands of students" and "over a decade" cannot be independently verified. The complete absence of any external mentions (news, social media, testimonials beyond the website) is unusual.
  1. Pop-psychology packaging: The "conscious and unconscious mind" framing, "animal behavior theory" applied to humans, and "hidden capabilities" language are markers of the coaching/self-help industry rather than evidence-based educational programming.
  1. Budget concentration: 256K NIS flowing to a single individual with no organizational accountability structure deserves scrutiny from the Gefen program evaluation system.
  1. Dual-market positioning: The same content appears to be marketed to both the Gefen system (where it is tagged with anti-racism/activism themes) and to Derech Ami (a nationalist Jewish identity organization). The ability to sell the same product to ideologically different buyers may indicate the content is so generic it can be framed either way.

Open Questions for Further Investigation

  1. What is the actual contact-hour count per school? 25.6K NIS/school needs context — is this 2 workshops or 20?
  2. Does Kaspi employ subcontractors? The facilitator guide on Amiway's site suggests others may deliver his program.
  3. What is the Derech Ami relationship specifically? Is he a paid vendor, a volunteer content partner, or something else?
  4. Why has no school ever publicly referenced this program? With 10 schools and thousands of claimed students, the absence of ANY external mention is striking.
  5. What specific anti-racism outcomes are measured? The Gefen rating of 3/5 suggests MoE evaluators found the program adequate but not exceptional — on what criteria?
  6. What is his tax registration number? This would enable checking the actual revenue flow through obudget.org or the tax authority.
