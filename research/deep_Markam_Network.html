2. What Is "Mixed Communities"? (Entity A Analysis)

"Mixed" means religious-secular Jewish coexistence — specifically dati (religious/observant) and hiloni (secular) Jews choosing to live together in the same settlements/neighborhoods.

This is NOT:

  • Jewish-Arab coexistence (that would be Hand in Hand / יד ביד)
  • Mixed socioeconomic (that would be urban renewal initiatives)
  • Mixed Ashkenazi-Mizrahi (no such framework exists formally)

The movement emerged from:

  • Gesher (גשר) — a decades-old organization bridging religious-secular divides
  • Tzav Piyus (צו פיוס) — reconciliation initiative, led by Aliza Gershon
  • Founded after community members from both movements discovered growing tensions between religious and secular neighbors and decided to create a formal support network

Over 20 communities participate in the network, including:

  • Tzofim (near Qalqilya)
  • Shalphim (Meiyot Valley)
  • Aliyav (Lachish region)
  • Various urban communities

Core philosophy: People with different religious lifestyles can share communities, education, and celebrations. Not about compromise but about active coexistence — secular families participate in communal Shabbat meals; religious families accept that neighbors may drive on Shabbat.

3. The "סיור פני אדם" (Human Faces Tour) Program

What It Is

An immersive audio headphone tour inside the former "Pussycat" (הפוסיקט) strip club at the Colosseum building, Kikar Atarim (Atarim Square), Tel Aviv.

How It Works

  1. Students receive individual headphones
  2. They enter the three-story building, including underground levels
  3. Audio installation created by artist Alit Kraiz (עלית קרייז), based on a concept by "Otzarot" (אוצרות) company
  4. The audio tells stories of women who worked at the strip club, from multiple perspectives (workers, customers)
  5. Different audio tours are provided for male and female participants (gender-separated content)
  6. Students walk through the actual rooms where the club operated
  7. Post-tour processing workshop addresses:
  • Choice vs. coercion
  • Healthy sexuality (מיניות בריאה)
  • Boundaries in digital and physical spaces
  • Gender equality
  • Social responsibility and involved citizenship

Target Audience (from Gefen)

  • Grades 9-13 (high school)
  • IDF soldiers
  • Parents
  • Education staff and administrators

Program Duration

1.5-2 hours depending on group size

Building History

  • The Colosseum building at Kikar Atarim housed the "Pussycat" strip club from 2012-2019
  • In 2019, JTLV real estate company (led by Amir Biram, Ariel Roter, investor Shalom Fisher) purchased the property and shut down the club
  • JTLV donated the building for social use
  • "המרחב החברתי" (The Social Space) opened in 2020 as a social incubator and visitor center
  • Crowdfunding campaign "Rising from the Ruins" (קמים מהחורבות) helped launch it
  • Approximately 1,200 weekly visitors as of latest data
  • ~2,500 monthly visitors including corporate, government, and educational tourism groups

7. Gender Content — Why Flagged?

The Flagging Mechanism

In the Gefen scoring model, program #31655 was flagged because:

  1. Themes detected: gender, activism, violence_prevention, sexuality, disability_inclusion
  2. The gender+sexuality theme combination has a 19x lift for R4+5 programs (the strongest predictor of ideological content in the entire model)
  3. Rating source: org_inherited:מרקם - רשת הקהילות המעורבות — meaning the rating was inherited from the organization name, not from direct investigation of the program content

What the Program Actually Contains

The program explicitly teaches:

  • "Healthy sexuality" (מיניות בריאה) — including discussion of choice, consent, boundaries
  • Gender equality — the former strip club as evidence of patriarchal exploitation
  • Anti-prostitution / anti-trafficking framing — women who worked at the club are presented as victims of systemic exploitation
  • Digital responsibility — connecting to social media pressures on youth
  • Social activism — encouraging students to become "involved citizens"

Why This Is Contentious

  1. Taking minors (ages 14-18) into a former strip club and exposing them to narratives about sex work, even in an "educational" framework
  2. Gender-differentiated tours — male and female students hear different audio content, raising questions about what messaging each gender receives
  3. The program presents a specific ideological position on prostitution (abolitionist/radical feminist) as though it were the only perspective
  4. Post-tour workshops on "healthy sexuality" with teenagers in a loaded environment
  5. The program name is misleading: "סיור פני אדם" (Human Faces Tour) sounds like a generic cultural tour; parents may not realize it involves sexuality content in a former strip club

Rating Assessment

The 4/5 rating inherited from the org name is somewhat misdirected (it was inherited from "מרקם - רשת הקהילות המעורבות" which is a different entity). However, based on the actual program content — explicit gender/sexuality education with an ideological frame, delivered in a provocative setting — a rating of 3-4 is appropriate on content grounds alone.

10. Open Questions for Further Investigation

  1. Why does Entity C (כל אחד ממקומו) use the supplier name "מרקם" in the Gefen catalog? This creates confusion with Entity A (מרקם - רשת הקהילות המעורבות). Is this a deliberate brand strategy, a legacy name, or an error?
  1. What is the financial flow between Entity B (580354538) and Entity C (515497121)? They share board leadership but are separate legal entities.
  1. What did Entity B (למרחב לשינוי חברתי) do from 2000 to 2019? 19 years of existence before the Social Space opened.
  1. Who are the private donors providing ~NIS 3.2M/year to Entity B? 95% of revenue is non-governmental and non-transparent.
  1. Is board member Eyal Biram related to JTLV's Amir Biram? If so, the building "donation" may involve conflicts of interest.
  1. Are there documented parent complaints about the sexuality content for minors?
  1. What exactly is different between the male and female audio tours? The gender-differentiated content raises pedagogical questions.
  1. Does Entity A (the real מרקם, 580541910) know that another entity is using their brand name in the Ministry of Education system?
